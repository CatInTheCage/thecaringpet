New Cat Owner Checklist
Your first 30 days with a new cat — printable guide

Before You Bring Your Cat Home

Food & Water

  • Food bowls (ceramic or stainless steel)
  • Water bowl or fountain
  • Cat food (same brand as shelter/breeder)
  • Healthy treats

Comfort & Safety

  • Covered bed or hiding box
  • Scratching post (vertical + horizontal)
  • Breakaway collar + ID tag
  • Carrier
  • Microchip registered to you

Litter & Cleanup

  • Litter box (large, uncovered)
  • Unscented clumping litter
  • Litter scoop
  • Litter mat
  • Enzyme cleaner

Play & Enrichment

  • Wand toy
  • Puzzle feeder or treat ball
  • Cat tree or window perch

Optional

  • Automatic feeder
  • Feliway diffuser
  • Grooming brush & nail trimmers
  • Pet camera
Quick tip: Start with the same food the shelter or previous owner used. Transition to a new brand gradually over 7–10 days to avoid stomach upset.

The 3-3-3 Rule
What to expect in your cat's first 3 months
3 Days — Decompress
Your cat is overwhelmed. Give them a small, quiet safe room and let them hide.
  • Keep in one safe room with the door closed
  • Let the cat exit the carrier on their own
  • No forced interaction or handling
  • Same food, fresh water, clean litter
3 Weeks — Settle In
Routine starts to feel familiar. Your cat's real personality begins to emerge.
  • Gradual, supervised exploration beyond the safe room
  • Establish consistent feeding and play times
  • Bond through short play sessions and treats
  • Watch appetite, litter habits, and energy
3 Months — Full Trust
The cat treats the home as territory and feels like part of the family.
  • Full home access if the cat is confident
  • Behavior patterns are stable
  • Begin slow introductions to other pets (if any)
  • Continue daily play and enrichment
Remember: Every cat is different. Shy or previously traumatized cats may take longer — that's normal, not a sign something is wrong. Let your cat set the pace.

When to Call the Vet Immediately

  • Not eating for more than 24 hours
  • No urination in 24 hours, or straining in the litter box
  • Vomiting or diarrhea lasting more than a day
  • Lethargy, weakness, or difficulty breathing
  • Sudden behavior change combined with refusing food
