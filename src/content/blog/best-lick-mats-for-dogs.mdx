Spread a thin layer of peanut butter, yogurt, or wet food across the textured surface, and a 30-second treat becomes a 10-minute calming activity. ## How We Chose the Best Lick Mats for Dogs

We scored every mat on six criteria tied to safety, effectiveness, and real-world durability:

1. **Material safety.** We prioritize food-grade, BPA-free silicone or non-toxic TPR rubber. Mats must be free of PVC, phthalates, and toxic fillers.
2. **Durability.** Lick mats are for licking, not chewing. We favor thicker materials, reinforced edges, and "Tuff" versions for dogs that might mouth the mat.
3. **Texture design.** Crosshatch patterns work best for thick spreads; brush-tip "soother" patterns are better for liquids and purees. We note which texture suits which food.
4. **Stability.** Suction cups or a heavy, non-slip base prevent the mat from sliding across the floor during enthusiastic licking.
5. **Ease of cleaning.** Bacteria and biofilm grow quickly on food residue. We prioritize dishwasher-safe mats with textures that rinse clean.
6. **Value.** Price is judged against expected lifespan, included accessories, and whether the product is sold as a multi-pack.

A product had to score at least 4 out of 5 on safety and 3.5 overall to make our shortlist.

## Lick Mat Buying Guide

### Choose the right texture

Lick mats come in three main surface patterns, each suited to different foods:

- **Crosshatch / Buddy:** Best for thick spreads like peanut butter, mashed banana, cream cheese, and wet food.
- **Brush-tip / Soother:** Best for liquids, broth, gravy, purees, and yogurt because the fine bristles hold runny food.
- **Waffle / grid:** A middle ground that works with most consistencies.

If you plan to freeze the mat, choose a crosshatch or grid pattern. Liquid fillings in brush-tip mats can become difficult to lick once fully frozen.

### Match the material to your dog

- **TPR rubber (LickiMat):** Slightly firmer, more textured, and generally more durable than basic silicone. The Tuff line adds a hard base that resists bending.
- **Food-grade silicone:** Softer, more flexible, and often sold with suction cups. Ideal for sticking to walls but easier for chewers to damage.

Avoid mats that do not specify food-grade or BPA-free materials. Cheap PVC mats can leach plasticizers and may have a strong chemical smell.

### Decide if you need suction cups

Suction cups are useful for:
- Bathing and grooming
- Sticking to crate walls
- Keeping the mat in place on smooth floors

They are not necessary for calm dogs that lick in place, and they will not adhere to porous or textured surfaces.

### Size matters

Most standard lick mats are 20 × 20 cm (8 × 8 inches). This size works for most dogs from small breeds up to large breeds. Giant breeds may finish a standard mat quickly; for them, use two mats or pair lick-mat enrichment with a slow feeder bowl from our [best slow feeder for large dogs](/blog/best-slow-feeder-for-large-dogs/) guide.

## How to Use a Lick Mat Safely

Lick mats are safe for most dogs when used correctly. Follow these veterinary-aligned guidelines:

1. **Supervise first-time use.** Watch how your dog interacts with the mat. Remove it if your dog starts chewing rather than licking.
2. **Use pet-safe fillings.** Avoid xylitol-containing peanut butter, chocolate, grapes, raisins, onions, garlic, and salty spreads. Plain yogurt, xylitol-free peanut butter, canned pumpkin, mashed banana, and wet dog food are safe choices.
3. **Freeze for longer enrichment.** Freezing a filled mat extends the activity from a few minutes to 15–30 minutes and is especially helpful during storms or departures.
4. **Clean after every use.** Rinse or wash the mat after each session. Most silicone and rubber mats are top-rack dishwasher safe.
5. **Inspect regularly.** Replace the mat if you notice tears, missing pieces, or deep tooth marks that could harbor bacteria or be swallowed.
6. **Not a meal replacement.** Lick mats are enrichment, not a complete feeding strategy. For daily meals, see our guide to [why dogs eat too fast](/blog/why-does-my-dog-eat-so-fast/) and how to slow them down.

## Best Foods to Spread on a Lick Mat

- **Xylitol-free peanut butter** — check the label carefully
- **Plain Greek yogurt** — high in protein, but avoid flavored or sweetened varieties
- **Canned pumpkin puree** — not pumpkin pie filling
- **Mashed banana or cooked sweet potato**
- **Wet dog food or raw food**
- **Low-sodium bone broth** — best for brush-tip / Soother-style mats

For anxious dogs, pair the lick mat with a calming pre-departure routine. The ASPCA recommends giving long-lasting food toys only when you leave and removing them when you return, so the toy becomes a predictor of calm alone time.
