## Quora 红线（先读，避免封号）

1. **不要只发链接**。Quora 会把"纯链接回答"折叠甚至删号。回答主体必须是**实质内容**，链接放在末尾"延伸阅读"，且最好自然融入一句话。
2. **回答长度 150–300 字**，太短被判低质，太长没人看。
3. **第一句话直接回答问题**，不要寒暄、不要"Great question!"。
4. **一个新号先纯回答 3–5 个问题不带链接**，养出"被看过/被赞同"记录，再开始带链接。带链接后保持 80% 回答无链接 / 20% 带链接的比例。
5. **链接必须语境匹配**：问的是"large dog slow feeder"就链对应文章，绝不乱链。
6. **不要复制粘贴整段文章**到 Quora——Google 会判重复内容，反而害你自己的文章。写**精简版 + 独特表述**。

## 模板 1：best slow feeder for large dogs

**适用问题**（Quora 常见问法）：
- What's the best slow feeder for a large dog?
- Best slow feeder bowl for Labradors / German Shepherds?
- My big dog eats too fast, which slow feeder works?

**回答模板**：

> For a large breed, the key isn't just "any slow feeder" — it needs enough capacity (2–4 cups), a heavy non-slip base so it doesn't get pushed across the floor, and a maze that's challenging without being so deep your dog gives up. Lightweight plastic bowls fail on big dogs because they flip them or slide them within a meal.
>
> Vets recommend slow feeders for large breeds partly because fast-eating large dogs are at higher risk of GDV (gastric dilatation-volvulus, or bloat), which is a life-threatening emergency. Slowing intake down by even 30–60 seconds per meal meaningfully reduces that risk.
>
> Pick one sized for your dog's muzzle and daily portion, not just the "large" label on the box.
>

## 模板 2：why does my dog eat so fast

**适用问题**：
- Why does my dog eat so fast?
- Is it dangerous if my dog eats too fast?
- How do I stop my dog from inhaling his food?

**回答模板**：

> Dogs eat fast for three main reasons: instinct (competition-driven scavenging instincts from their ancestry), behavioral (learned from littermate competition or food insecurity), and sometimes medical (parasites or GI issues — worth a vet check if the speed is new and extreme).
>
> The real concern isn't the speed itself but what it causes — choking, vomiting, and for large deep-chested breeds, bloat (GDV), which can kill within hours. That's why vets take fast eating seriously in big dogs.
>
> The most effective fix is a slow feeder matched to your dog's size, plus splitting meals into two and removing competition. Puzzle feeders work for dogs that need mental engagement too.
>

## 模板 3：ceramic vs plastic slow feeder / bowl safety

**适用问题**：
- Is ceramic or plastic better for a dog bowl?
- Are plastic slow feeders safe?
- Which dog bowl material is healthiest?

**回答模板**：

> Ceramic is generally the safer long-term choice. Plastic bowls develop microscopic scratches over time that trap bacteria, and some dogs develop contact allergies (nasal dermatitis) from plastic. Ceramic is heavier, doesn't slide, and survives dishwasher sanitizing cycles.
>
> The trade-offs: ceramic breaks if dropped, and not all ceramic is food-safe — cheap unglazed ceramic or chipped glaze can leach lead. Look for FDA/food-grade glazed ceramic and replace it the moment it chips.
>
> Stainless steel is actually the vet-preferred middle ground: durable, dishwasher-safe, no leaching. But between ceramic and plastic specifically, ceramic wins on hygiene and safety.
>

## 模板 4：new cat / puppy owner checklist

**适用问题**：
- What do I need before getting a cat / puppy?
- First time cat owner, what should I buy?
- How to prepare for a new puppy?

**回答模板**：

> Before bringing the pet home, focus on the essentials that prevent the first-week disasters: food + bowls appropriate to the species/age, a carrier, litter box (cat) or crate (puppy), a safe confined space, and vet appointment booked in advance. Skip the toys and accessories until week one — you'll learn what the animal actually likes.
>
> The framework that helps most new owners is the 3-3-3 rule: 3 days to decompress, 3 weeks to learn the routine, 3 months to feel fully at home. Expect hiding and refusal to eat in the first 3 days — that's normal, not a problem.
>
> Print a checklist so you're not relying on memory in the stressful first days.
>

## 模板 5：how to clean slow feeder / cat bowl hygiene

**适用问题**：
- How do you clean a slow feeder bowl?
- How often should I wash my dog's bowl?
- My dog's bowl smells, is it making him sick?

**回答模板**：

> Slow feeders need more than a quick rinse — the grooves trap food residue, saliva, and bacteria that a sponge can't reach. A daily quick clean plus a weekly deep clean is the standard vets recommend.
>
> Daily: rinse immediately after the meal and scrub grooves with a bottle brush or dedicated bowl brush. Weekly: soak in hot soapy water, scrub every ridge, and run it through the dishwasher if it's dishwasher-safe (the sanitize cycle kills most bacteria). Plastic goes in the top rack; check ceramic for chips each time.
>
> A biofilm or sour smell means you're overdue — dirty bowls are linked to recurrent GI and skin issues in pets.
>
